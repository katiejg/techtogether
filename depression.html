What is Depression

Depression is characterized by longer and/or deeper feelings of sadness/hopelessness

Signs of Depression

How to tell if you or other people near you are depressed

  • Hopelessness
  • Frequent Tearfulness
  • Fatigue all the time
  • Changed Sleep Pattern
  • Change in Appetite
  • Feelings of worthlessness & Self-Blame
  • Thoughts of suicide
  • Unrealistic ideas and/or worries

Treatments

Although Depression may seem to get the best of you sometimes, there are many healthy ways to help yourself or others.

  • Mental Health Professionals
    • Clinical Psychologists
    • Therapists
    • Religious Counselors
  • Antidepressants
    • Make sure to consult a trusted doctor before taking any medication
  • Group Therapy
    • Sharing stories often helps
      • Hearing others who are going through depression or went through a phase(s) of depression can help both you and the other people

What can Depression lead to?

Depression can lead to many serious mental and physical illnesses. To learn more about that, click here. However, depression is most known for creating suicidal thoughts.

Suicide

Suicide is the second leading cause of death in young people and most of the time, it is caused by depression

People who consider suicide often confide in someone. If someone confides in you, make sure to pay attention and show that you care. If you feel uncomfortable at any point, please contact a professional or use one of the many resources on our resources page. It is crucial for the person to get help. Whether it is a fleeting thought or a frequent thought, suicidal thinking should be taken seriously.

Signs of Suicidal Thoughts

  • Being depressed or other mental disorders
  • Talking/Implying the desire to be dead
  • Social isolation
  • Significant changes in appearance, lack of hygiene
  • Making preparations for death - e.g. giving away possesions, reckless spending of money, etc.
  • Mood changes

Causes of Suicide or Suicidal Thoughts

  • Previous suicide attempts
  • Having close friends, relatives or other members of the community who committed suicide
  • Cultural religious beliefs that support suicide
  • Alcohol or drug abuse
  • Hopelessness
  • Watching or experiencing neglect or violence
  • Traumatic experiences
  • Access to means of suicide
  • Unwillingness to seek help

Ways to help Suicide

If you suspect/know someone who is considering suicide, there are many ways to help them.

  • Listen to the person if they confide in you and show that you care and empathize with them.
  • Tell a trusted adult or consult a professional
  • Change can be slow, but persist
  • If you feel like you will burn out soon, contact a professional for help.
  • Look at a resource listed in our resources page
  • If you know someone who plans to commit suicide, call 911 immediately
